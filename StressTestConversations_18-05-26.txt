Te Papa Tongarewa Museum of New Zealand is New Zealand's national museum, right on the Wellington waterfront in Central Wellington. It's one of the country's must-visit cultural landmarks.

Highlights
Treaty of Waitangi exhibition — explore the founding document of modern Aotearoa
Gallipoli exhibition (by Weta Workshop) — an extraordinarily immersive WWI experience
Rongomaraeroa marae — a living, contemporary Māori meeting place inside the museum
Māori artworks and carvings, plus New Zealand natural history displays
Good to know
Hours: Open daily 10am–6pm (closed Christmas Day)
Getting there: 20-minute walk along the waterfront from Wellington's central train station
Tip: Allow at least 2 hours — you could easily spend a full day here
Great for first-time visitors to NZ, Māori culture enthusiasts, history lovers, and families
Cafes, a gift shop, and guided tours available on-site; some exhibitions are paid
Pair it with
After Te Papa, locals recommend strolling along the waterfront to Oriental Bay or heading up Mount Victoria for panoramic views — making for a fantastic full-day Wellington itinerary.

🌿 A Day at Pūrākaunui Falls — The Catlins
Base: Owaka (the main service town, 15 km away) | Drive style: Southern Scenic Route

🕘 9:00 am — Pūrākaunui Falls
Kick off early before the crowds arrive. The 20-minute rainforest walk winds through lush bush to a gorgeous three-tiered, 20-metre waterfall — one of the most photographed in New Zealand. There's a wheelchair-accessible top viewing platform, a lower platform via stairs, and picnic tables at the car park.

📸 Tip: Visit after recent rain for the most dramatic flow. Check kōtukutuku flowering August–December for a bonus flora display.
🕙 10:00 am — Pūrākaunui Bay
Just a short drive from the falls, this wild, remote bay has dramatic cliffs, a 500-metre beach, and excellent wildlife spotting. Great for a stroll along the shore and some seal-watching.

🕚 11:00 am — Helena Falls Beach
A hidden gem — a secluded golden-sand beach with its own waterfall tumbling nearby. Keep an eye out for yellow-eyed penguins, fur seals, and sea lions. Short walk from the parking area.

🕛 12:30 pm — Lunch in Owaka
Owaka is the Catlins' main hub — grab lunch here. It has a café and bakery for a quick refuel. (Restaurant content isn't indexed in full detail yet, but Owaka's main street is your best option in the area.)

🕑 2:00 pm — Catlins Lake & Pounawea
Head to the Catlins River estuary near Pounawea — a peaceful bird sanctuary with walking tracks, fishing spots, and stunning reflective water views. Perfect for a post-lunch wander.

🕒 3:30 pm — Cannibal Bay
A dramatic remote beach with golden sands and reliably excellent sea lion sightings. Keep your distance from the wildlife and soak up the raw, untouched coastal scenery.

🕓 5:00 pm — Wrap up / head to accommodation
Return to Owaka or continue along the Southern Scenic Route toward Balclutha or Invercargill.

🌲 Whakarewarewa Forest and Scion
Location: Rotorua, Bay of Plenty

A beloved outdoor destination established in the late 1800s to trial exotic tree species for New Zealand's forestry industry. Today it's one of the country's most popular multi-use forests, home to towering redwoods, sequoias, and Monterey pines.

Highlights:

Redwood Memorial Grove — stunning stands of giant coastal redwoods
Redwoods Treewalk — a famous elevated walkway through the redwood canopy
Scion Research Institute — photo galleries and educational exhibits on NZ forestry history, plus a café
Redwoods Visitor Centre — barista coffee, snacks, maps, gift shop; open daily (except Christmas)
Activities:

Walking, running, mountain biking, horse trekking
Exploring Scion's exhibits
Good to know:

Dogs are not allowed (registered service dogs need advance notice for the Treewalk)
Gates open from 5:30am to late at the main entry; walk/bike access is available anytime
Accessible by City Ride bus (route 3 Owhata) or a short drive from the city
